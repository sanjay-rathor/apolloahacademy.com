Frequently Asked Questions

What are the eligibility criteria for this program?
Applicants must have completed 10+2 or equivalent with Physics, Chemistry, and Biology as compulsory subjects with a minimum of 50% marks in aggregate from a recognized board. Students from PCM background with Biology as an additional subject are also eligible.
How is the program structured?
The 3-year program is divided into six semesters. The first year covers foundational sciences, the second year focuses on core laboratory techniques, and the third year includes specialized diagnostic procedures and clinical internships. Each semester has both theory and practical components.
Are scholarships available?
Yes, Apollo University offers merit-based scholarships covering up to 40% of tuition fees for outstanding applicants. Additionally, need-based financial aid and education loans with partner banks are available. Students from economically disadvantaged backgrounds may qualify for special scholarships.
What kind of placement support is provided?
Our dedicated Career Development Cell offers comprehensive placement support including resume building, interview preparation, and hospital networking opportunities. We conduct clinical laboratory recruitment drives with 25+ institutions visiting campus. Our 92% placement rate reflects our strong industry connections with hospitals and diagnostic centers.
What certifications are included in the program?
The program includes preparation for national and international certifications like DMLT (Diploma in Medical Laboratory Technology) and ASCP (American Society for Clinical Pathology) international certification. Students also receive additional certifications in specialized techniques like PCR, ELISA, and Flow Cytometry.
Can I specialize in a specific laboratory field?
Yes, in the fifth and sixth semesters, students can choose electives from specialized tracks including Clinical Biochemistry, Hematology, Microbiology, Histopathology, or Molecular Diagnostics. Your final research project and internship can also be aligned with your specialization interest.
What is the fee payment schedule?
Fees are payable semester-wise. The first installment is due at the time of admission, and subsequent payments are due at the beginning of each semester. We offer flexible payment options including semester-wise, annual, or one-time full payment with applicable discounts.
What are the higher education opportunities after this program?
Graduates can pursue M.Sc in Medical Laboratory Technology, Clinical Research, Biochemistry, Microbiology, or other allied health sciences. Many of our students go on to specialize through post-graduate diplomas in specialized areas like Molecular Biology, Cytogenetics, or Blood Banking. About 30% of our graduates pursue higher education within 2 years of graduation.
