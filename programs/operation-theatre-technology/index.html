Frequently Asked Questions

What are the eligibility criteria for this program?
Applicants must have completed 10+2 or equivalent with Physics, Chemistry, and Biology as main subjects with at least 50% marks (45% for reserved categories) from a recognized board. Selection is based on entrance test performance and personal interview.
How is the program structured?
The 3-year program consists of six semesters with a mix of theoretical classes, laboratory sessions, and clinical postings. The final year includes extensive hospital rotations and a six-month internship in various specialty departments. Students also complete a clinical project in their final semester.
Are scholarships available?
Yes, Apollo University offers merit-based scholarships covering up to 30% of tuition fees for outstanding applicants. Additionally, need-based financial aid and education loans with partner banks are available. Students from underprivileged backgrounds may qualify for special scholarships through our Inclusive Education Initiative.
What kind of placement support is provided?
Our dedicated Placement Cell provides comprehensive career support including resume building, interview preparation, and clinical skills assessment. We conduct dedicated healthcare recruitment drives with Apollo Hospitals and other partner institutions. Our program maintains a 90% placement rate, with many students receiving offers during their internship period.
Is there any entrance examination?
Yes, qualified applicants are required to take the Apollo Allied Health Sciences Entrance Test (AAHSET) which tests basic knowledge in biology, chemistry, physics, and general aptitude. Shortlisted candidates are then called for a personal interview before final selection.
What clinical exposure will I receive?
Students get extensive clinical exposure starting from the second semester. This includes observation in operating theatres, hands-on training in simulation labs, and progressive responsibility in clinical settings. By the final year, students rotate through general surgery, orthopedics, cardiac, neuro, pediatric, and emergency departments with supervised participation in actual procedures.
What is the fee payment schedule?
Fees are payable semester-wise. The first installment is due at the time of admission, and subsequent payments are due at the beginning of each semester. We offer flexible payment options including semester-wise, annual, or one-time full payment with applicable discounts for full payment.
What higher education options are available after this program?
Graduates can pursue higher studies including M.Sc in Operation Theatre Technology, M.Sc in Perfusion Technology, M.Sc in Critical Care, or specialized PG Diplomas. Those interested in research can also explore M.Phil or Ph.D programs in related biomedical fields. Additionally, various specialty certifications in advanced anaesthesia techniques are available for career advancement.
