Frequently Asked Questions

What are the eligibility criteria for this program?
Applicants must have completed 10+2 (Higher Secondary) with Physics, Chemistry, and Biology subjects with a minimum of 50% marks from a recognized board. Candidates should be at least 17 years of age at the time of admission.
How is the program structured?
The 3-year program consists of six semesters. The first year focuses on foundational medical sciences, the second year on clinical skills and basic procedures, and the third year on specialty rotations and comprehensive clinical practice. The final semester includes mandatory internship at Apollo hospitals.
Are scholarships available?
Yes, Apollo University offers merit-based scholarships covering up to 30% of tuition fees for outstanding applicants. Additionally, need-based financial aid, education loans with partner banks, and special scholarships for students from rural and underserved areas are available.
What kind of placement support is provided?
Our dedicated Career Development Cell offers comprehensive placement support including resume building, interview preparation, and clinical skills enhancement. We conduct healthcare recruitment drives with Apollo Group and other partner hospitals. Our placement rate is over 90%, with many students receiving pre-placement offers during their internship.
What is the difference between a Physician Assistant and a Doctor?
Physician Assistants are trained healthcare professionals who work under the supervision of physicians. Unlike doctors who complete MBBS and can practice independently, PAs support physicians in patient care, clinical procedures, and healthcare delivery. The education duration is shorter (3 years vs. 5.5+ years for doctors), but PAs play a crucial complementary role in the healthcare team.
Can I specialize in a specific medical field?
Yes, in the third year, students can choose elective clinical rotations in areas such as Cardiology, Orthopedics, Pediatrics, Emergency Medicine, or Surgery. While the B.Sc degree is general, these focused rotations help you develop specialized skills and may provide advantage when seeking employment in specialty departments.
What is the fee payment schedule?
Fees are payable annually or semester-wise. The first installment is due at the time of admission, and subsequent payments are due at the beginning of each academic year or semester as chosen. We offer flexible payment options including EMI schemes through our partner banks.
What higher education options are available after this program?
Graduates can pursue M.Sc in Physician Assistant Studies, Master's in Hospital Administration, or other allied health specializations. Some universities also offer bridge courses for B.Sc PA graduates to enter medical education programs. Additionally, specialized certifications in areas like Emergency Medicine, Critical Care, or Surgical Assistance can enhance career prospects.
