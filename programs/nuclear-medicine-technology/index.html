Frequently Asked Questions

What are the eligibility criteria for this program?
Candidates must have completed 10+2 (Higher Secondary) with Physics, Chemistry, and Biology/Mathematics with a minimum aggregate of 50% marks from a recognized board. Students from Science backgrounds are preferred.
How is the program structured?
The 3-year program includes six semesters of academic study combined with clinical rotations. The final semester includes extensive internship and project work in nuclear medicine departments. Each year builds on the previous one, beginning with foundational knowledge and progressing to advanced clinical applications.
Are scholarships available?
Yes, Apollo University offers merit-based scholarships covering up to 30% of tuition fees for outstanding applicants. Additionally, need-based financial aid and education loans with partner banks are available. Special scholarships are also available for students from economically disadvantaged backgrounds.
What kind of placement support is provided?
Our dedicated Placement Cell organizes campus interviews with leading hospitals, diagnostic centers, and research institutions. We provide resume building, interview preparation, and hospital-specific training. Our program has a 90% placement rate with strong connections to Apollo Hospitals Group and other major healthcare providers.
What is the scope of nuclear medicine technology?
Nuclear Medicine Technology is a rapidly growing field with excellent career prospects. With the increasing use of PET/CT and other nuclear imaging techniques in diagnosis and treatment monitoring, qualified technologists are in high demand. Career advancement opportunities include specialization in PET/CT, nuclear cardiology, or moving into education and research.
What clinical exposure will I get during the program?
Students receive over 1000 hours of clinical training across the three years, including rotations in PET/CT, conventional nuclear medicine, radiopharmacy, and therapeutic nuclear medicine. Clinical rotations take place at Apollo Hospitals and other partner institutions equipped with state-of-the-art nuclear medicine facilities.
What is the fee payment schedule?
Fees are payable semester-wise. The first installment is due at the time of admission, and subsequent payments are due at the beginning of each semester. We offer flexible payment options including semester-wise or annual payment with applicable discounts for full annual payment.
Are there opportunities for higher studies after this program?
Graduates can pursue M.Sc in Nuclear Medicine Technology, Radiation Physics, or Medical Imaging Technology. Many of our students also go on to specialized PG Diploma programs in PET/CT Technology or Nuclear Cardiology. Some graduates have successfully pursued higher education abroad in countries like the US, UK, Australia, and Canada.
