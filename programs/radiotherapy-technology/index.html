Frequently Asked Questions

What are the eligibility criteria for this program?
Applicants must have completed 10+2 (Higher Secondary) with Physics, Chemistry, and Biology/Mathematics with at least 50% marks from a recognized board. Selection is based on entrance test performance and personal interview. Basic computer knowledge is preferred but not mandatory.
How is the program structured?
The 3-year program consists of six semesters. Each year includes both theoretical coursework and practical/clinical training. The final semester focuses heavily on hospital-based internship and a research project. Students spend approximately 60% of their time in practical sessions and clinical rotations.
Are scholarships available?
Yes, Apollo University offers merit-based scholarships covering up to 40% of tuition fees for outstanding applicants. Additionally, need-based financial aid and education loans with partner banks are available. Special scholarships are also available for students from economically disadvantaged backgrounds.
What kind of placement support is provided?
Our dedicated Placement Cell provides comprehensive support including resume building, interview preparation, and campus recruitment drives. Apollo Hospitals Group offers priority consideration for our graduates. We maintain a 90% placement rate, with many students receiving offers during their final semester internship.
Is there any radiation exposure risk during training?
Student safety is our top priority. All clinical training follows strict radiation safety protocols with proper supervision. Students are provided with dosimeters to monitor exposure levels, and our labs have advanced safety features. Comprehensive radiation safety training is provided before clinical rotations begin.
What advanced technologies will I learn to operate?
Students receive training on Linear Accelerators, CT Simulators, Treatment Planning Systems, Brachytherapy units, and various imaging modalities. Our labs feature equipment from major manufacturers like Varian, Elekta, and Siemens. Students also gain experience with specialized software used in radiation oncology departments.
What is the fee payment schedule?
Fees are payable semester-wise. The first installment is due at the time of admission, and subsequent payments are due at the beginning of each semester. We offer flexible payment options including semester-wise, annual, or one-time full payment with applicable discounts.
What are the career advancement opportunities after this course?
Graduates can pursue higher education through M.Sc in Medical Physics, Radiation Physics, or Medical Imaging Technology. Many opt for specialized certifications in advanced techniques like IGRT, IMRT, or Stereotactic Radiotherapy. Some graduates also pursue opportunities abroad where there is high demand for qualified radiation therapists.
