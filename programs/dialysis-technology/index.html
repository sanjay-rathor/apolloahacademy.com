Frequently Asked Questions

What are the eligibility criteria for this program?
Applicants must have completed 10+2 or equivalent with a minimum of 50% marks in Physics, Chemistry, and Biology/Mathematics. Students from any stream (Science/Commerce/Arts) with Psychology as a subject are also eligible. Admission is based on merit and performance in the entrance examination.
How is the program structured?
The 3-year program is divided into six semesters. The first year focuses on foundational psychology and human biology, the second year covers advanced psychological concepts and health applications, while the third year emphasizes clinical skills, specialized topics, and a research project with clinical placements.
Are scholarships available?
Yes, Apollo University offers merit-based scholarships covering up to 40% of tuition fees for academically outstanding applicants. Additionally, need-based financial aid and education loans with partner banks are available. Students from underrepresented backgrounds may qualify for special scholarships.
What kind of placement support is provided?
Our Career Development Cell offers comprehensive placement support including resume building, interview preparation, and networking opportunities. We conduct healthcare psychology job fairs with employers from hospitals, clinics, research organizations, and NGOs. Our program maintains an 85% placement rate within 6 months of graduation.
Can I pursue higher studies after this program?
Absolutely. Graduates can pursue M.Sc in Psychology, Clinical Psychology, Health Psychology, or related fields. Many of our graduates have been accepted into prestigious universities for their master's programs both in India and abroad. The program provides a solid foundation for specialized higher education.
What clinical exposure will I receive during the program?
Students receive 600+ hours of supervised clinical experience across various settings including hospitals, mental health clinics, rehabilitation centers, and community health organizations. Clinical placements begin in the fourth semester and continue through the final year, providing diverse patient exposure and practical skill development.
What is the fee payment schedule?
Fees are payable semester-wise. The first installment is due at the time of admission, and subsequent payments are due at the beginning of each semester. We offer flexible payment options including semester-wise, annual, or one-time full payment with applicable discounts.
What research opportunities are available for students?
All students complete a guided research project in their final year. Additionally, we offer research assistantships with faculty members working on funded projects. Our Psychology Lab is equipped with assessment tools, biofeedback equipment, and software for psychological research. Exceptional student research is supported for presentation at conferences.
