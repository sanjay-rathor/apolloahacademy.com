Frequently Asked Questions

What are the eligibility criteria for this program?
Candidates must have completed 10+2 or equivalent with Physics, Chemistry, and Biology as core subjects with a minimum aggregate of 50%. Selection is based on entrance examination performance and personal interview. Basic knowledge of mathematics is recommended.
How is the program structured?
The 3-year program consists of six semesters with increasing clinical exposure each year. The first year focuses on basic sciences and fundamentals, the second on specialized critical care knowledge and skills, and the third on advanced techniques and internship. Each semester includes both theoretical learning and practical training.
Are scholarships available?
Yes, Apollo University offers merit-based scholarships covering up to 40% of tuition fees for outstanding applicants. Need-based financial aid and education loans with partner banks are also available. Additionally, we have special scholarships for students from underrepresented regions and backgrounds.
What kind of placement support is provided?
Our Career Development Cell provides comprehensive placement support including resume building, interview preparation, and clinical skills workshops. We conduct healthcare recruitment drives with 25+ hospitals and medical technology companies. Our program boasts a 92% placement rate within three months of graduation.
What certifications will I receive along with the degree?
In addition to your B.Sc degree, you'll receive preparation and support for Basic Life Support (BLS), Advanced Cardiac Life Support (ACLS), and Pediatric Advanced Life Support (PALS) certifications. These internationally recognized certifications enhance your employability globally.
What specialized training facilities are available?
Our campus features a state-of-the-art Critical Care Simulation Center with high-fidelity patient simulators, ventilators, hemodynamic monitors, and emergency equipment. We also have specialized labs for ventilator management, dialysis technology, and advanced cardiac monitoring. Students have access to Apollo Hospitals' ICUs for clinical rotations.
What is the fee payment schedule?
Fees are payable semester-wise. The first installment is due at the time of admission, and subsequent payments are due at the beginning of each semester. We offer flexible payment options including semester-wise, annual, or one-time full payment with applicable discounts.
What career advancement opportunities exist after this program?
Graduates can pursue M.Sc in Critical Care, Perfusion Technology, or Respiratory Therapy. Advanced diploma courses in specialized areas like ECMO Technology, Cardiac Critical Care, or Neurocritical Care are also options. Many graduates advance to supervisor roles within 3-5 years of experience or pursue teaching positions at healthcare institutions.
